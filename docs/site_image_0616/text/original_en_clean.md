### XO-001 (TNIK Inhibitor)

**Target**

- TNIK (Traf2 and NCK-interacting kinase)

**Pathway**

- Wnt/beta-catenin Signaling

**Therapeutic Impact**

- Tumor Growth Suppression
- Anti-Metastatic Activity
- Cancer Stem Cell Suppression
- Resistance Prevention

**Differentiation**

- Terminal Wnt Transcription Control
- Selective Targeting of APC-Mutant Tumors
- Reduced On-Target Toxicity
- Prevention of Recurrence & Resistance

**Why XO-001?**

- Terminal Wnt Control  
  XO-001 selectively targets TNIK, the terminal regulator of Wnt/beta-catenin signaling.
- Enhanced Selectivity  
  Selective inhibition of aberrant Wnt signaling while minimizing toxicity to normal tissues.
- Durable Disease Control  
  Suppresses cancer stem cell-driven metastasis, recurrence, and therapeutic resistance.

### XO-003 (FOXM1 Inhibitor)

**Target**

- FOXM1

**Pathway**

- FOXM1-Driven Transcriptional Program

**Therapeutic Impact**

- Proliferation Suppression
- Anti-Metastatic Activity
- Resistance Prevention
- Cellular Senescence Induction

**Differentiation**

- Master Transcription Factor Targeting
- Multiple-Pathway Suppression
- Resistance Mechanism Blockade
- Combination Therapy Potential

**Why XO-003?**

- Master Regulator Targeting  
  Targets FOXM1, a central transcriptional driver of cancer progression.
- Multi-Pathway Suppression  
  Simultaneously inhibits proliferation, metastasis, angiogenesis, and drug resistance.
- Combination Therapy Potential  
  Enhances therapeutic response by overcoming resistance to standard treatments.

## Page 4. Technology Continued

### XO-004 (IRP2 Inhibitor)

**Target**

- IRP2

**Pathway**

- IRP2-Mediated Iron Homeostasis

**Therapeutic Impact**

- Iron Deprivation
- Mitochondrial Dysfunction
- Autophagic Cell Death
- Tumor Growth Suppression

**Differentiation**

- Metabolic Vulnerability Targeting
- Cancer-Selective Iron Disruption
- Reduced Impact on Normal Cells
- Activity in Drug-Resistant Tumors

**Why XO-004?**

- Iron Dependency  
  Exploits the elevated iron demand required for cancer cell proliferation.
- Cancer Selectivity  
  Disrupts iron homeostasis in tumor cells while sparing normal cells.
- Drug-Resistant Tumors  
  Demonstrates efficacy in tumors resistant to conventional targeted therapies.

### XOA-001 (ADC Platform)

**Platform**

- ADC Platform

**Payload**

- KS-10070 (XO-002)

**Therapeutic Impact**

- Selective Tumor Cell Killing
- Bystander-Mediated Tumor Clearance
- Enhanced Intratumoral Drug Exposure
- Reduced Systemic Toxicity

**Differentiation**

- Enhanced Tumor Internalization
- Optimized Linker Stability
- Potent KS-10070 Payload
- Strong Bystander Effect

**Why XOA-001?**

- Enhanced Internalization  
  Optimized tumor cell uptake and intracellular delivery.
- Stable Linker Design  
  Maintains stability in circulation to reduce off-target toxicity.
- Potent Payload  
  KS-10070 enables strong tumor cell killing at low concentrations.
- Bystander Effect  
  Diffuses to neighboring tumor cells to overcome tumor heterogeneity.
